Unit 1.2: The Greek Alphabet
Home > Unit 1 > Unit 1.2

It seems strange and exotic, but the Greek alphabet is actually very similar to our own alphabet, which was derived from it. You should pick it up quickly, especially if you say the words out loud. The major difference from our writing system is that Greek has diacritical marks: a series of punctuation like accents, breathing marks, and the like. These marks were put in place by the 2nd century BCE in order to help pronounce and distinguish words. Original Greek pronunciation was based on pitch rather than stress, which has a sing-song effect, like in modern Italian today. Remember that you are not trying to reproduce Greek words, but to recognize and analyze them. Accents can help you, but do not let an obsession over them bog you down.
