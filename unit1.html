Unit 1: Introduction

Learn about grammar, the Greek

alphabet and digital tools.

Home > Unit 1

This unit introduces you to the methods and tools we will use inthis coursein lieu of a textbook. You will learn the terminology of grammar and dependency syntax. You will also familiarize yourself with the Greek alphabet. Finally, you will be presented with the various digital tools that are freely available to you on the internet.

Unit 1.1
Grammar: The Metalanguage of Language

In order to learn to read Greek, you must first develop a strong grammatical foundation. If you are going to talk about a language, you need to know the language of language. In particular, this course will be using grammatical trees built on the rules of something called Dependency Syntax, a simple framework for discussing the structure of sentences. These lessons will teach you how to talk about language, using English for all the examples.

Unit 1.2
The Greek Alphabet

It seems strange and exotic, but the Greek alphabet is actually very similar to our own alphabet, which was derived from it. You should pick it up quickly, especially if you say the words out loud. The major difference from our writing system is that Greek has diacritical marks: a series of punctuation like accents, breathing marks, and the like.

Unit 1.3
Digital Tools

Various tools are freely available on-line to help you instantly identify the form, definition, and function of words within a sentence.
